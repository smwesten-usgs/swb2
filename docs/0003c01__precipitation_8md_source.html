16 Method of Fragments
17 ===================
18 
19 The method of fragments creates synthetic sequences of daily rainfall
20 from monthly rainfall by imposing the rainfall pattern from selected
21 rain gages with daily data (see, for example,
22 Srikanthan and McMahon (1999)). The synthesized daily rainfall data
23 approximate the long-term average character of daily rainfall, such as
24 frequency, duration, and intensity, but may not necessarily reproduce
25 the actual historical daily rainfall record. Rain gages should be
26 selected on the basis of location, and the length and completeness of
27 daily records. Thiessen polygons are drawn around each of the selected
28 rain gages, and the daily rainfall pattern within each Thiessen
29 polygon
30 is assumed to be the same as the pattern at the rain gage.
31 
32 Daily rainfall fragments are calculated by dividing each daily
33 rainfall
34 measurement for a particular month by the total rainfall measured at
35 the
36 gage for that month. This results in a set of fragments for that
37 particular month in which the total number of fragments is equal to
38 the
39 number of days in the month. Fragment sets are compiled for every
40 selected gage for every month in which complete daily rainfall
41 measurements are available. Fragment sets are grouped by month of the
42 year and by rain gage. In the water-balance calculation, the fragment
43 set used for a given gage for a given month is selected randomly from
44 among all available sets for that gage for that month. Daily rainfall
45 for a given month is synthesized by multiplying total rainfall for
46 that
47 by each fragment in the set.
