1 Model Theory {#model_theory}
2 ------------------------------
3 
4 The SWB code uses a modified Thornthwaite-Mather soil-moisture
5 accounting method (Thornthwaite and Mather, 1957) to calculate
6 recharge; recharge is calculated separately for each grid cell in the
7 model domain. Sources and sinks of water within each grid cell are
8 determined on the basis of input climate data and landscape
9 characteristics; recharge is calculated as the difference between the
10 change in soil moisture and these sources and sinks.
11 
12 ![Figure 1:
13 Conceptual_diagram_SWB_simplified.png](images/Conceptual_diagram_SWB_simplified.png)
14 
15 The water balance components used to calculate potential recharge
16 amounts are shown schematically in fig. 1.
17 
18 The calculation proceeds as follows:
19 
20 1. Precipitation is partitioned into gross rainfall and/or gross
21  snowfall,
22 
23 2. Intercepted rain or snow is added to the interception storage
24  reservoir,
25 
26 3. Net snowfall is added to the snow storage reservoir,
27 
28 4. Snowmelt (if any) is calculated,
29 
30 5. Potential evapotranspiration (PET) is calculated,
31 
32 6. Interim soil moisture is calculated as
33  $\\theta_{interim}=\\theta_{t-1}+rainfall+snowmelt+runoff-runon$,
34 
35 7. Interim soil moisture fraction is calculated as:
36  $f = \\frac{{\\left( {{\\theta *{interim}} - {\\theta
37  *{wilting\\;point}}} \\right)}}{{\\left( {{\\theta
38  *{field\\;capacity}} - {\\theta *{wilting\\;point}}} \\right)}}$,
39 
40 8. Actual evapotranspiration (AET) is calculated as some function of
41  $f$ and $PET$,
42 
43 9. Updated soil moisture is calculated as
44  $\\theta_t=\\theta_{t-1}+rainfall+snowmelt+runon-runoff-AET$,
45 
46 10. If the updated soil moisture ($\\theta_t$) exceeds the field
47  capacity
48  of the soil, the updated soil moisture is set to
49  $\\theta_{field\\;capacity}$, making the change in soil moisture
50  $\\Delta\\theta=\\theta_{field\\;capacity}-\\theta_{t-1}$,
51 
52 11. If the updated soil moisture is less than the field capacity,
53  potential recharge is considered to be zero,
54 
55 12. Otherwise, potential recharge is calculated as:
56  $potential\\;recharge=\\theta_t-\\theta_{field\\;capacity}$.
57 
58 In some cases it might be useful to simulate recharge in urban areas
59 in
60 a more detailed manner. This option is triggered in SWB 2.0 when a
61 percent or fraction impervious area grid is supplied to the code
62 (fig. 2). When this option is active, an additional storage reservoir
63 is
64 created: "impervious surface storage". In addition, it is possible to
65 take storm drains into account by supplying the fraction of impervious
66 surface storage that is intercepted by storm drains.
