1 Crop Coefficients {#crop_coefficients}
2 ========================================
3 
4 When the irrigation demand is simulated in SWB, the underlying
5 soil-moisture module is automatically changed to the FAO-56 The
6 calculation under 'nonstandard' conditions includes the
7 transpiration-limiting effects of soil water stress on plants. When
8 this
9 option is invoked in swb, the Thornthwaite-Mather soil moisture
10 retention tables are **not** consulted. Rather, the crop
11 evapotranspiration amount $(E{T_{c,adj}})$ is adjusted by
12 incorporation
13 of a water stress factor $({K_s})$ whose value may range between 0.0
14 and
15 1.0:
16 
17 $$E{T_{c,adj}} = {K_s}{K_c}E{T_{o}}$$
18 
19 ${K_s}$ is defined by the doil moisture deficit relative to two soil
20 moisture amounts: the Readily Available Water (RAW) and Total
21 Available
22 Water (TAW) amounts. At soil moisture deficits less than the RAW
23 amount,
24 it is assumed that plants have adequate available moisture for growth;
25 plants are assumed to be under no water stress. The value of ${K_s}$
26 is
27 one under these conditions.
28 
29 Once soil moisture deficit increases beyond the RAW amount, ${K_s}$
30 decreases linearly, reaching a value of zero as the soil moisture
31 deficit approaches the TAW value.
32 
33 Total Available Water $(TAW)$ is defined as the **maximum** amount of
34 water that can be present within the root zone, and is calculated in
35 swb
36 as:
37 
38 $$TAW = AWC \\cdot (rooting\\;depth)$$
39 
40 where
41 
42 $AWC$ is the Available Water Capacity, in inches per foot, and\\
43 $root\\;depth$ is the current rooting depth of vegetation in feet.
44 
45 Readily Available Water $(RAW)$ is defined as the amount of water that
46 can be withdrawn by a plant from soil moisture storage without the
47 plant
48 suffering water stress. $RAW$ may be defined as some fraction of the
49 Total Available Water:
50 
51 $$RAW = p \\cdot TAW$$
52 
53 where
54 
55 $p$ is the fraction of Total Available Water $TAW$ that can be removed
56 from soil moisture storage before a plant begins suffering from water
57 stress.
58 
59 $p$ is called the "plant stress depletion fraction" in the swb
60 irrigation lookup table.
